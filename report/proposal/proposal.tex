\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
\noindent Promptable trackers such as SAM\,3~\cite{sam3} segment and track any named object in a
video with no training on that category. Conservation teams now point them at new species in new
places constantly --- but nobody can say, \emph{in advance}, whether the model is trustworthy for a
given animal in a given location. This project builds and validates a \textbf{predictive rule} for
that reliability. Using SA-FARI~\cite{safari} --- whose train/test splits are disjoint by
\emph{species and location} --- we measure SAM\,3's zero-shot promptable-tracking accuracy with the
\emph{official} evaluator, decompose it into \textbf{detection} (\texttt{pDetA}) and
\textbf{association} (\texttt{pAssA}) via the HOTA family~\cite{hota}, and ask whether each can be
predicted from four \textbf{label-free distances} (taxonomic, visual, environment, temporal) computed
against the training split alone. The heavy model is frozen throughout; only a small statistical model
is fit. Predictable out-of-distribution generalisation is established for \emph{classifiers}
\cite{aotl,agl} but not for trackers; our contribution is the first such study for a \emph{promptable
video tracker}, decomposed into two predictors, yielding a deployment ``will it work here?'' estimator
with honest confidence intervals. The result is informative either way.
\end{abstract}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Background and motivation}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------
A \textbf{promptable tracker} is a vision--language model that, given a text prompt like
\texttt{"impala"}, segments and tracks every matching animal across a video with no training on that
species (zero-shot). SAM\,3~\cite{sam3} is the current exemplar. This capability is transforming
camera-trap ecology: a single model can be aimed at any species anywhere. But it creates a new,
unanswered question --- \emph{when should we trust it?} Teams deploy these models on unseen species
and regions with no rule that predicts, before running, whether the output is reliable. That gap is
what we fill.

The idea that generalisation can be \emph{predicted} is not new for classifiers. ``Accuracy on the
Line''~\cite{aotl} showed that, across models and shifts, out-of-distribution accuracy correlates
strongly and linearly with in-distribution accuracy; ``Agreement on the Line''~\cite{agl} made the
estimate \emph{label-free} by using inter-model agreement. Both concern classification. Two further
results motivate \emph{which} properties should predict transfer here: vision foundation models carry
taxonomic structure across the tree of life (BioCLIP~\cite{bioclip}), motivating taxonomic/visual
distance; and camera-trap recognition degrades sharply at new locations (Terra
Incognita~\cite{terra}), motivating an environment distance. What is missing is any predictable-transfer
study for a \emph{promptable video tracker}, and any treatment of the fact that tracking has two
distinct failure modes --- failing to \emph{find} the animal, and failing to \emph{follow} it --- that
may be governed by different factors.

\textbf{The metric makes that decomposition clean.} Promptable HOTA (pHOTA) factors, HOTA-style
\cite{hota}, into \texttt{pDetA} (detection: did it find the animals?) and \texttt{pAssA} (association:
did it keep each identity over time?). Analysing the two \emph{separately} is the intellectual core of
this proposal: we hypothesise they transfer for different reasons.

\textbf{SA-FARI is a natural experiment.} The largest open multi-animal wildlife tracking dataset
(Meta $\times$ Conservation X Labs)~\cite{safari} spans \textbf{99 species} with a full 7-level
taxonomy, \textbf{741 locations} across four continents, and a decade of footage. Crucially, its
train and test splits are \textbf{disjoint by species \emph{and} location}, so every test video is a
controlled probe of transfer along a \emph{measurable} distance from the training set.

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Research question and hypotheses}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------
\noindent\textbf{RQ.} Can we predict SAM\,3's zero-shot \texttt{pDetA} and \texttt{pAssA} on an unseen
(species, place) \emph{before running it}, from properties measurable in advance --- and which factors
govern transfer, separately for finding the animal and following it?
\begin{description}[leftmargin=2.6em,style=nextline]
  \item[H1 (detection)] \texttt{pDetA} falls with \textbf{species novelty} --- taxonomic and visual
    distance from the nearest training species.
  \item[H2 (association)] \texttt{pAssA} falls with \textbf{environment difficulty} --- scene distance,
    day/night/IR, clutter, camera motion --- largely regardless of species.
  \item[H0 (null, still a result)] the distances explain little variance $\Rightarrow$ transfer is not
    simply distance-driven; pivot to representational probing (does SAM\,3 encode taxonomy?).
\end{description}
\noindent\textbf{Success} is a \emph{validated, out-of-sample} predictor of \texttt{pDetA}/\texttt{pAssA}
from label-free distances, with honest confidence intervals --- \emph{not} a higher tracking score.

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Proposed approach}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------
\paragraph{Measurement.} We run \textbf{frozen} SAM\,3 over the SA-FARI test split (species-specific
prompts as the primary condition; a generic ``animal'' prompt for robustness), and score with the
\textbf{official VEval} evaluator --- \emph{the metric is never re-implemented}. Scores are aggregated
to a \textbf{cell} = a \texttt{(species, location\_id, time)} group, carrying \emph{support} counts
($n_\text{frames}$, $n_\text{masklets}$, $n_\text{videos}$). Hard negatives (a query returning nothing)
are kept --- they drive false-positive analysis.

\paragraph{Four label-free distances} (all computed against the \emph{frozen} training set, using no
label of the target species): \textbf{taxonomic} (tree steps to the nearest training species via the
lowest common ancestor); \textbf{visual} (cosine distance of DINOv2~\cite{dinov2} embeddings of the
\emph{mask-cropped} animal to the nearest training-species prototype); \textbf{environment} (scene
embedding of the animal-masked-out background to the nearest training location, plus a night/IR flag);
and \textbf{temporal} (years to the nearest relevant training footage). A \textbf{SAM-3 familiarity
proxy} --- separability of the test species in SAM\,3's own feature space --- hedges against the fact
that SAM\,3's true pretraining corpus is unknown; a CLIP~\cite{clip} variant of the visual distance is
a robustness check.

\paragraph{Fitting the law.} We model each bounded target with \textbf{beta regression / logit-link
GLM}, \emph{weighted by support} and including a $\log(n_\text{frames})$ covariate so that ``rare'' is
never mistaken for ``far''. Variance is attributed with dominance/commonality analysis (not four
univariate fits) with collinearity (VIF) controlled. The predictor is then validated
\textbf{out-of-sample} by holding out \emph{whole species and whole locations} in cross-validation, and
packaged as a reliability estimator: four distances in $\rightarrow$ predicted \texttt{pDetA}/\texttt{pAssA}
$+$ interval out.

\section{Novelty (stated honestly)}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------
The contribution is \emph{not} that OOD performance can be predicted (established for
classifiers~\cite{aotl,agl}), nor that embeddings carry taxonomy~\cite{bioclip}, nor that camera-trap
models shift by location~\cite{terra}. It is the \emph{combination}: to our knowledge, the \textbf{first
predictable-transfer study for a promptable video tracker}, \textbf{decomposed into two predictors}
(detection vs association, via HOTA), on SA-FARI's species\,$\times$\,place\,$\times$\,time natural
experiment --- yielding a practical \emph{deployment reliability estimator}.

\section{Evaluation, success criterion and constraints}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------
\paragraph{Out-of-sample validation.} The headline is not a within-sample fit but a \emph{predictor}:
leave-one-species-out and leave-one-location-out cross-validation (whole groups held out) predict
\texttt{pDetA}/\texttt{pAssA} from distances alone, reported with bootstrap confidence intervals and
calibration. Two decision gates keep the project honest: (i) after measurement, are the scores in
SA-FARI's SAM\,3 ballpark? (ii) after validation, is there real out-of-sample signal --- if not, invoke
H0 and pivot to representational probing.

\paragraph{Hard constraints.} Freeze the seen set \emph{first} and compute every distance against the
training split only (no test information may enter a feature --- leakage would make the ``prediction''
trivial). Use the \emph{official} evaluator for all metrics. Model bounded scores with support-weighted
beta/logit GLMs. Hold out whole species and locations. Keep hard negatives. Inference-only: a single
modern GPU suffices.
