\section{Research design and evaluation criteria}
\label{sec:meth-design}
% NOTE: This section (new) frames the whole study and states the DECISION RULE up front, so that a
% null is a principled outcome measured against a pre-stated bar rather than a failed experiment.
This work is a predictive-modelling study: we ask whether label-free \emph{distances} measurable in
advance ($X$) predict a frozen tracker's zero-shot performance ($Y=$ \texttt{pDetA}/\texttt{pAssA}), and
which factors govern that transfer for \emph{finding} versus \emph{following} an animal. Because the
target is a bounded score and the observations are grouped by species and location, the analysis is a
weighted regression with group-aware, out-of-sample validation rather than a tuned tracker.

\paragraph{Definition of success and the decision rule.}
% NOTE: state this BEFORE any results appear. This is the single most important paragraph for making
% H0 read as a result. Adjust wording to taste but keep both criteria (interval + out-of-sample).
The intended deliverable is a \emph{validated, out-of-sample} predictor of \texttt{pDetA}/\texttt{pAssA}
from label-free distances, with honest confidence intervals. A distance is taken to predict transfer only
if it satisfies \textbf{both} criteria: (i) its coefficient's group-cluster-bootstrap 95\% confidence
interval excludes zero, \emph{and} (ii) the grouped cross-validated model beats a mean-predictor baseline
by a margin that is significant under a paired group-bootstrap. \textbf{H1} (detection $\leftarrow$
species novelty) and \textbf{H2} (association $\leftarrow$ environment difficulty) are supported only when
their respective distances meet this bar on the split that isolates them; if neither does under these
deliberately powerful tests, we conclude \textbf{H0} --- distance explains little variance --- which is
itself a valid result and motivates the representational-probing direction of Chapter~\ref{ch:discussion}.

\section{Dataset, splits, and the analysis unit}
\label{sec:meth-data}
We use SA-FARI~\cite{safari} throughout. In the released \texttt{\_ext} annotations, a probe is a
(video, text-prompt) pair; the animal's species and its seven-level taxonomy are resolved through the
prompt's \texttt{category\_id}, while per-video \texttt{location\_id} and creation timestamp give place and
time. We aggregate scores to a \emph{cell} --- a (species, location, time) group --- and attach support
counts ($n_\text{frames}$, $n_\text{masklets}$, $n_\text{videos}$) used for weighting and as covariates.
Hard negatives (prompts for absent species) are kept, as they drive the false-positive side of detection.

\paragraph{Two complementary splits.}
Inspection of the annotations shows the shipped split is disjoint by \emph{location} but shares species
across train and test (Section~\ref{sec:bg-safari}); on that split species-distance is $\approx 0$ for every
cell, so it cannot test species novelty. Because SAM\,3 is frozen and zero-shot on all of SA-FARI, the split
only defines our \emph{reference distribution}, so we are free to repartition the same videos. We therefore
use two near-orthogonal experiments and run inference only once (scoring is per-video and split-agnostic):
\begin{description}[leftmargin=1.6em,style=nextline]
  \item[Split A --- species hold-out (primary).] Pooled species are partitioned by a \emph{leave-one-species-out}
    view, so a held-out species' distance is to the nearest \emph{other} species; species-distance varies while
    the environment stays largely familiar, isolating H1. Validated by leave-species-out cross-validation.
  \item[Split B --- location hold-out (secondary).] The official train/test split, whose unseen locations
    isolate H2 and whose benchmark comparability carries the Phase-1 score-sanity check.
\end{description}
Split A is a \emph{constructed} hold-out --- legitimate because the model is frozen, and reported as such.

\section{The frozen reference (leakage firewall)}
\label{sec:meth-reference}
For each split the reference (``seen'') species, locations, and taxonomy are frozen once; every distance is
then computed against that reference only, so no probe information can enter a feature --- verified by a unit
test that swaps the reference and checks the distances move as expected. We assert the axis that is genuinely
disjoint (location for Split~B; the held-out species for Split~A) and \emph{report} any residual species
overlap honestly rather than asserting it away.

\section{Label-free distance features}
\label{sec:meth-features}
All distances are label-free for the target and computed against the frozen reference.
\subsection{Taxonomic distance}
Lowest-common-ancestor tree steps from the probe's species to the nearest reference species (shared genus
$=1$, family $=2$, order $=3$, \dots); the leave-species-out reference keeps this non-trivial for shared
species.
\subsection{Visual distance}
Cosine distance between DINOv2~\cite{dinov2} embeddings of the \emph{ground-truth-mask-cropped} animal and
the nearest reference-species prototype, with a CLIP~\cite{clip} encoder as a robustness check. Using the
dataset's ground-truth masks means this feature needs no SAM\,3.
\subsection{Environment distance}
Embedding distance between the animal-masked-out background scene and the nearest reference location, plus an
interpretable day/night--infrared flag and a clutter score derived from colour statistics.

\section{Statistical modelling and hypothesis testing}
\label{sec:meth-model}
% NOTE: this is the analytical core and where the H0 methodology lives. Expand each paragraph below.
\subsection{Per-target regression}
Each bounded target (\texttt{pDetA}, \texttt{pAssA}) is modelled \emph{separately} with a support-weighted
logit-link (fractional-logit) regression over the standardised distances plus the scene covariates and a
$\log(n_\text{frames})$ covariate, so that ``rare'' is never mistaken for ``far''. Detection and association
are analysed apart because their decomposition is the intellectual core of the project.

\subsection{Honest, group-aware inference}
% NOTE: this paragraph turns your engineering rigor into a methods strength. Keep the WHY (why naive CIs lie).
Naive model confidence intervals are invalid here for two reasons: support-weighting by frame count inflates
the effective sample size, and the distances are constant within a species or a location, so the hundreds of
cells are really only tens of independent groups (pseudo-replication). We therefore report
\emph{group-cluster-bootstrap} intervals --- resampling whole species and whole locations, refitting, and
taking percentile intervals --- as the honest confidence interval for every coefficient.

\subsection{Out-of-sample validation and the significance test}
The fitted models are validated \emph{out of sample} by holding out whole groups (leave-species-out on
Split~A, leave-location-out on Split~B) and predicting the held-out group. The out-of-sample gain over a
mean-predictor baseline is tested with a \emph{paired group-bootstrap} that resamples whole held-out groups,
yielding a confidence interval and a one-sided $p$-value on the gain --- the criterion~(ii) of
Section~\ref{sec:meth-design}.

\subsection{Variance attribution and confound control}
Variance is attributed to each factor by dominance/commonality analysis with a VIF collinearity check --- not
by four univariate fits --- and raw correlations are reported as a model-free cross-check. Where a distance
reaches significance, we refit with the animal-size covariate to test whether the effect is a size confound
rather than novelty.

\subsection{The intrinsic-difficulty pivot and the nested decomposition}
Because the sole apparent effect proved to be a size confound, we ask a complementary question: is transfer
governed by the intrinsic \emph{difficulty} of the target imagery rather than its \emph{novelty}? We promote
the label-free scene properties already measured for the environment axis --- low-light/IR
(\texttt{achromatic\_fraction}), clutter, and the night/IR flag --- from nuisance covariates to predictors of
interest, and test them against the same out-of-sample bar. To separate a genuine difficulty effect from the
size confound, we fit a \emph{nested} sequence of models --- support only; size only; difficulty only (no
size); difficulty plus size --- and compare their out-of-sample gains, so that any apparent difficulty effect
that is really size is exposed as such (Chapter~\ref{ch:results}, Table~\ref{tab:difficulty}). We also report
each scene property's \emph{cell-level} correlation alongside its location-level value, because aggregation
inflates correlations (the ecological-correlation fallacy).

\paragraph{Detecting a signal, not asserting its absence.}
% NOTE: pre-empts the "your null is just low power" objection. Keep it.
These tests are demonstrably able to detect a real effect: the animal-size covariate not only has an interval
excluding zero but \emph{validates out of sample} at the same whole-group hold-out bar the distances fail
(Chapter~\ref{ch:results}), and the estimation and cross-validation machinery is unit-tested to recover
planted effects on synthetic data. A null under a test that fires for size is therefore evidence of
\emph{absence of signal}, not of \emph{absence of power}.

\section{Detection precision: false positives on hard negatives}
\label{sec:meth-fp}
% NOTE: new section. The pDetA fit conditions on the animal being present (recall-given-present); this is the
% precision counterpart. Fill in once the hard-negative pass has scored (still running at time of writing).
The \texttt{pDetA} model conditions on the animal being present, so it measures recall-given-present, not
hallucination. On hard negatives (the queried species is absent) a correct run returns no masklet; we read
the prediction directly, flag any returned masklet above a score threshold as a false positive, attach each
species' taxonomic and visual distance, and test --- at the species level, to avoid pseudo-replication ---
whether the hallucination rate rises with novelty.

\section{Reproducibility, implementation, and constraints}
\label{sec:meth-repro}
The model is frozen and scored by the official evaluator; the seen set is frozen before any distance is
computed; hard negatives are retained; and all inference is single-GPU and inference-only. The pipeline is
config-driven (every constraint is a toggle, so an ablation flips config, not code) and covered by hermetic
tests; every reported number is regenerated from the repository, and split definitions, seeds, and reference
sets are persisted so both experiments are reproducible end to end.
