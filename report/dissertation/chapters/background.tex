The HOTA family~\cite{hota} decomposes tracking accuracy into detection and association components ---
the split that is the intellectual core of this work.

\section{Predictable out-of-distribution generalisation}
% TODO: established for classifiers; not for trackers.
``Accuracy on the Line''~\cite{aotl} and ``Agreement on the Line''~\cite{agl} show OOD accuracy is
predictable (and label-free) for \emph{classifiers}; no analogue exists for promptable video trackers.

\section{What should predict transfer}
% TODO: motivate the four distances.
Vision foundation models carry taxonomic structure~\cite{bioclip} (motivating taxonomic/visual
distance), and camera-trap recognition degrades at new locations~\cite{terra} (motivating environment
distance). We embed animals/scenes with DINOv2~\cite{dinov2}, with CLIP~\cite{clip} as a robustness variant.

\section{The SA-FARI benchmark}
% TODO: scale, taxonomy, locations, disjoint splits, hard negatives.
SA-FARI~\cite{safari} spans 99 species (7-level taxonomy), 741 locations across four continents, with
train/test disjoint by species \emph{and} location --- a controlled transfer probe.
