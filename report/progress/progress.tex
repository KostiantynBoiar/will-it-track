\section{Context}
The project asks whether SAM\,3's zero-shot promptable-tracking accuracy on an unseen (species, place)
can be \emph{predicted before running the model}, from label-free distances, and \emph{which} factors
govern that transfer --- separately for \textbf{finding} the animal (\code{pDetA}) and \textbf{following}
it (\code{pAssA}). The deliverable is a validated, out-of-sample reliability estimator, not a higher
tracking score. This note reports what has been implemented against that plan; all code is committed to
the project repository and is reproducible from it.

\section{The plan, in brief}
Frozen SAM\,3 is scored by the official evaluator to give the dependent variables (\code{pDetA} /
\code{pAssA}); four \textbf{label-free distances} (taxonomic, visual, environment, temporal) form the
predictors; a small support-weighted beta regression is fit and validated out-of-sample. Two
complementary experiments isolate the two hypotheses: a \textbf{species hold-out} (leave-one-species-out,
the primary novelty axis) and the official \textbf{location hold-out} (the environment axis). The work is
split into six phases: setup and reference, measurement, distance features, fitting the law, out-of-sample
validation, and the reliability tool plus write-up.

\section{Implementation status}
The entire \textbf{label-free predictor side is complete and validated}, and the \textbf{measurement leg
is implemented and awaiting a GPU run}; the modelling half is scaffolded and unblocks once the scores exist.

\section{Key findings and design decisions}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The split premise was corrected.} Inspecting the released annotations showed the official
    SA-FARI split is disjoint by \emph{location}, but the \emph{species are largely shared} across it
    (only a handful of genuinely unseen test species). Because SAM\,3 is frozen and zero-shot on the whole
    dataset, the split is only our reference anchor for distances --- which licenses a \emph{constructed}
    species hold-out for the novelty experiment. This is now the design of record, replacing the original
    ``disjoint by species and location'' assumption.
  \item \textbf{The species-novelty axis is usable.} Over the $99$ present species ($72$ with full
    taxonomy), the leave-one-species-out taxonomic distance spans $0$--$4$ steps with a healthy spread, and
    species vary largely independently of location --- so the two hypotheses can be tested separately.
  \item \textbf{The distances behave sensibly on real data.} Spot checks are as expected (e.g.\ visually
    distinct animals score farther; infrared night footage is flagged correctly), giving confidence before
    the scores arrive.
  \item \textbf{Related work is positioned.} Label-free performance prediction is established for
    classifiers and, very recently, static detectors/segmenters --- never for a promptable \emph{video
    tracker}, and never split into detection versus association: precisely the gap this work fills.
\end{itemize}

\section{Engineering quality}
The codebase is typed and configuration-driven (Pydantic\,v2), lints clean, and carries a growing test
suite (currently $29$ passing tests, with heavier data- and GPU-dependent checks gated behind skip
markers). The label-free features were refactored into a shared pipeline and verified to reproduce their
outputs \emph{exactly}; the measurement leg is built so that its one model/format-specific step is
isolated and confirmed when the GPU environment is set up. Every result is regenerable from the repository.

\section{Remaining work and next steps}
The immediate milestone is the \textbf{first decision gate}: run the frozen SAM\,3 inference on the test
split (on Colab Pro, following the project's run\-book), score with the official evaluator, and confirm the
aggregate numbers land in SA-FARI's published SAM\,3 range. Passing that gate opens the modelling half ---
fitting the detection-versus-association law, attributing variance to each factor, validating it
out-of-sample with grouped cross-validation and bootstrap intervals, and wrapping the fitted models into
the ``will it work here?'' reliability estimator. The label-free feature table can be assembled in parallel,
as it needs no further access. In short: the predictors and the measurement machinery are in place; the
next step is to generate the scores and begin fitting.
